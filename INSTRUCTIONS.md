# User Instructions

## NHS Retirement Planner with Cash Streams

This workbook helps NHS Pension Scheme members and other users model retirement income, pension benefits, savings, investments and future cash streams across different retirement scenarios.

## Basic Rule

**Only complete the blue cells.**

Blue cells are user input cells. All other cells may contain formulas, calculations or protected assumptions and should not be changed unless you understand the workbook structure.

## Step 1: Enter Personal Details

Complete the blue input cells with your retirement assumptions, including:

* Current age
* Planned retirement age
* Life expectancy
* Current salary (where applicable)
* Inflation assumptions

## Step 2: Enter Pension Information

Enter the relevant pension information in the blue cells, including:

* NHS Pension benefits
* State Pension assumptions
* Private pension income
* Pension commencement dates

Review all pension assumptions carefully before relying on the outputs.

## Step 3: Enter Savings and Investments

Complete the relevant blue input cells for your retirement assets, including:

* ISA balances
* Cash savings
* Investment accounts
* Other retirement capital
* Expected future contributions

## Step 4: Enter Future Cash Streams

Model any future one-off or recurring income, including:

* Inheritance
* Lump sums
* Property sales
* Other expected capital receipts
* Additional retirement income sources

This allows you to explore how future events may affect your retirement position.

## Step 5: Review the Summary

Once the blue cells have been completed, review the dashboard and summary outputs.

The workbook may show estimates such as:

* Total retirement income
* Annual retirement cash flow
* Income surplus or shortfall
* Estimated additional lump sum required
* Estimated additional monthly saving required
* Retirement scenario comparisons

## Important Notes

* Results are estimates only.
* Only edit the blue input cells.
* Do not overwrite formulas.
* Review all assumptions before relying on the outputs.
* Pension rules, taxation, inflation, investment returns and legislation may change over time.
